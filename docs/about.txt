  Barack Obama

   Basic Information
- **Full Name:** Barack Hussein Obama II
- **Born:** August 4, 1961, in Honolulu, Hawaii
- **Political Party:** Democratic
- **Family:** Married to Michelle Obama (n√©e Robinson); two daughters, Malia and Sasha

   Education
- Occidental College (1979-1981)
- Columbia University (B.A. in Political Science, 1983)
- Harvard Law School (J.D. magna cum laude, 1991)
  - President of the Harvard Law Review (first African American to hold this position)

   Professional Career
- Community Organizer in Chicago (1985-1988)
- Civil Rights Attorney
- Constitutional Law Professor at University of Chicago Law School (1992-2004)
- Illinois State Senator (1997-2004)
- U.S. Senator from Illinois (2005-2008)

   Presidential Terms
- **44th President of the United States** (2009-2017)
- First African American to serve as U.S. President
- Inaugurated on January 20, 2009
- Re-elected for second term in 2012

   Major Achievements as President
- Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act ("Obamacare")
- American Recovery and Reinvestment Act
- Dodd-Frank Wall Street Reform and Consumer Protection Act
- Ended U.S. military involvement in the Iraq War
- Ordered operation that killed Osama bin Laden (2011)
- Paris Climate Agreement
- Iran Nuclear Deal
- Normalized relations with Cuba
- Marriage equality support

   Post-Presidency
- Established the Obama Foundation
- Published memoir "A Promised Land" (2020)
- Produced films and series with Higher Ground Productions
- Continued public speaking and advocacy work

   Awards and Recognition
- Nobel Peace Prize (2009)
- Two Grammy Awards for audio versions of his books
- Named Time Person of the Year (2008, 2012)

   Books Authored
- "Dreams from My Father" (1995)
- "The Audacity of Hope" (2006)
- "Of Thee I Sing: A Letter to My Daughters" (2010)
- "A Promised Land" (2020)
- "Renegades: Born in the USA" (2021, with Bruce Springsteen)

   Legacy
Barack Obama is widely recognized for his historic presidency as the first African American president, his efforts on healthcare reform, economic recovery following the 2008 financial crisis, and his administration's focus on climate change initiatives. His presidency represented significant social progress while also facing substantial partisan challenges.
